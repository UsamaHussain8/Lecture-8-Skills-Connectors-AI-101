# Report Template

Use this structure. Drop sections that don't apply rather than padding them with filler.

---

## Multi-job search report

```markdown
# Job Match Report — <role focus>, <location/work model>
*Generated <date> · <N> roles reviewed · sources: <boards searched>*

## At a glance

| # | Role | Company | Location | Fit | Key blocker |
|---|------|---------|----------|-----|-------------|
| 1 | Backend Engineer | Acme | Remote (PK ok) | Strong | — |
| 2 | Java Developer | Beta | Dubai | Reach | 5y Spring required |

Sorted best fit first.

---

## 1. <Role> — <Company>

**Link:** <url> · **Posted:** <date or "not stated"> · **Location:** <x> · **Comp:** <if stated>
**Fit: <band>** — <one sentence on why>

**Strong matches**
- <Requirement> → <the exact resume evidence that proves it>

**Partial matches**
- <Requirement> → <what the user has instead, and the transfer argument they should make>

**Gaps**
- <Requirement> → <absent. Hard blocker or closable? If closable, how and how fast.>

**If you apply:** <2–3 lines — which bullets to lead with, what to add, whether to write a cover letter.>

*(repeat per role)*

---

## Cross-cutting analysis

### Gaps that keep appearing
| Missing skill | Appeared in | Effort to close |
|---|---|---|
| Spring Boot | 7 of 9 roles | 2–3 weeks + one portfolio project |

Ranked by frequency. This is the highest-leverage section — say plainly which one or two things unlock the most listings.

### Strengths you're underselling
- <Real experience the postings ask for that the resume buries or omits.>

### Resume fixes
Concrete and per-bullet, ordered by impact:
1. <Specific change, and why these postings make it matter.>

### Suggested next moves
- <Apply now / build first / broaden or narrow the search — with reasoning.>
```
